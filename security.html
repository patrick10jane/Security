Security

Close protection, private security, and bodyguards are all terms that are used to describe individuals or teams of individuals who are hired to provide security and protection services to individuals, organizations, or assets. However, these terms have slightly different connotations and can refer to different types of services.

Close protection refers to security measures that are put in place to protect an individual, typically a high-risk individual such as a politician, celebrity, or executive, from harm. Close protection is often provided by trained and experienced bodyguards who are responsible for the physical safety and security of the individual they are protecting.

Private security refers to the use of privately contracted security personnel for the protection of property, assets, or individuals. Private security companies can offer a wide range of security services, including close protection, surveillance, and security consulting.

Bodyguards are individuals who are specifically trained to provide close protection services to high-risk individuals. Bodyguards typically work as part of a close protection team and are responsible for the physical safety and security of the individual they are protecting. Bodyguards are often highly trained in personal protection techniques, firearms, and emergency response procedures.

In conclusion, close protection and bodyguards are similar in that they both refer to the protection of individuals, but the term "close protection" is more general and can refer to any measures taken to protect an individual, while "bodyguards" specifically refers to individuals who are trained to provide close protection services. Private security, on the other hand, refers to the use of privately contracted security personnel for the protection of property, assets, or individuals.
